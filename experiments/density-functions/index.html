Language Guide

now, this will probably be familiar if you've ever done javascript or whatever.. basically you type maths expressions like x/64+0.5 and such, and the results of your expression are displayed on the square image, with cyan being -1 and magenta being +1. the panel shows all values from x=0-127 and z=0-127, with 3d density functions not being properly visualized currently (y defaults to 64, so you can at least verify that the surface looks sort of normal, i guess)

this language also supports a few maths functions, taken largely from more density functions. do note, it's NOT comprehensive. you can complain to me about it or download this webpage locally and add them yourself..

notable wierdnesses

  • distance([x,y,z,...],[x,y,z,...]) - takes an abitrary set of two n-dimensional vectors, returning the euclidian distance between them.
  • perlin(x,z,xScale,zScale) - scale is extracted for compatability with vanilla. i know there's more noise functions also, sorry for not implementing them..
